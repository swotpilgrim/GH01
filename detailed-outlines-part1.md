# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: MOTOR CITY FURY (Chapters 1-15)

## Chapter 1: "The Fireball"

Target Word Count: 1,700 words
POV: Casey McKenna, first person
Setting: Abandoned factory roads, Detroit east side
Time: October 1977, Saturday night, 11pm

**Purpose:** Establish Casey's world and deliver devastating inciting incident (Danny's murder).

**Beats:**
- Cold open at illegal street race, Casey watching Danny lead in his '69 Charger, crowd electric
- Explosion mid-turn—fireball, car flips, crashes into concrete barrier, crowd scattering
- Casey runs to wreckage through flames, pulls Danny out, holds him as he dies in her arms
- Through tears she sees Johnny Calabrese across street in burgundy Cadillac, smoking cigar, smirking—makes eye contact, tips head mockingly
- After Danny dies, Casey examines wreckage while waiting for police—notices cut brake line, realizes it's murder

**Dialogue Topics:** Casey talking to Danny as he dies (reassurances, panic), brief exchanges with racing crew trying to pull her away, Casey's internal dialogue realizing brake line was cut professionally.

**Emotional Arc:** Starts with excitement and pride watching Danny race, crashes into horror and grief, ends with cold realization of murder—rage forming beneath shock.

**Key Details:** 1969 Dodge Charger specifications, smell of burning rubber and gasoline, October cold, Danny's last words to Casey, Calabrese's burgundy 1976 Cadillac Eldorado, cut brake line detail, racing crowd demographics (working-class Detroit, mixed ages).

## Chapter 2: "McKenna Blood"

Target Word Count: 1,700 words
POV: Casey McKenna, first person
Setting: McKenna's Garage, flashbacks to childhood/teenage years
Time: Three days after Danny's death, afternoon

**Purpose:** Establish Casey and Danny's relationship and skills through flashback, showing why revenge matters.

**Beats:**
- Present: Casey alone in garage three days post-funeral, can't work, staring at tools
- Flashback: Father Sean teaching them engines (ages 10 and 13), both kids learning carburetors and transmissions
- Flashback: First illegal races together as teenagers, Casey driving, Danny navigating, partnership forming
- Flashback: Danny at 20 going professional in underground circuit, Casey proud but staying in shadow as mechanic
- Recent memory: Week before death, Danny mentions Calabrese approached him to throw race, Danny refused publicly—Casey didn't think it mattered then

**Dialogue Topics:** Father's teaching style (gruff, hands-on, "figure it out"), Danny and Casey's sibling banter and inside jokes, Danny's refusal to Calabrese ("I don't throw races—tell your boss to bet on someone else"), Casey's supportive responses.

**Emotional Arc:** Grief and nostalgia for lost partnership, growing realization that Danny's refusal got him killed, survivor's guilt mixing with rage—she should have warned him.

**Key Details:** McKenna's Garage layout and smell (motor oil, metal, cigarettes), father's teaching methods, Danny's racing trophies on shelf, specific car models they built together, Detroit working-class culture, racing as family business.

## Chapter 3: "Evidence"

Target Word Count: 1,700 words
POV: Casey McKenna, first person
Setting: Police impound yard, McKenna's Garage, Danny's apartment
Time: Same day as Chapter 2, late afternoon into evening

**Purpose:** Casey investigates murder, gathers evidence, identifies saboteurs, realizes cops won't help.

**Beats:**
- Casey at police impound examining Charger wreckage—finds brake line cut clean (professional job), fuel system tampered with accelerant
- Confronts police detective who's dismissive ("Mechanical failure, racing's dangerous, case closed"), realizes cops won't investigate
- Returns to garage, talks with Tommy (mechanic, Danny's best friend)—he names saboteurs: Tony Rizzo and Mike Chen, known to do Calabrese's dirty work
- Tommy warns her: going after Calabrese is suicide, he owns cops and judges, controls entire racing circuit
- Casey visits Danny's empty apartment, finds racing trophies, childhood photos, blueprints for unfinished Camaro project—takes blueprints

**Dialogue Topics:** Police detective's condescending dismissal, Tommy's warnings and reluctance to name names (he's scared), Casey pressing for information, Tommy finally giving up saboteurs' names, Casey's quiet determination responses.

**Emotional Arc:** Methodical investigation mode masking grief, growing cold rage as evidence confirms murder, acceptance that legal system won't help—justice must be personal.

**Key Details:** Impound yard atmosphere (chain-link, guard shack, rows of wrecked vehicles), specific sabotage techniques visible on brake line and fuel system, Tommy's appearance (red-haired, hungover, loyal), Camaro blueprints detail, Danny's apartment frozen in time.

## Chapter 5: "Building War"

POV: Casey McKenna, first person
Setting: McKenna's Garage

**Beats:**

- Garage becomes armory: lays out weapons—father's Remington 870 shotgun (sawed-off), .38 revolver, boxes of ammunition, tire iron, road flares
- Test drives on empty factory roads at midnight—Camaro's a monster, fastest she's driven, practices high-speed maneuvers and ramming techniques on junked cars
- Final night: practices shooting at makeshift range, sleeps in garage with Camaro finished and weapons loaded

**Dialogue Topics:** Minimal dialogue—brief exchanges with father (mechanical instructions, grunts of approval), Tommy asking if she's sure about this path, Casey's clipped responses focused on specs and build details.

**Emotional Arc:** Cold methodical focus replacing grief, finding purpose in construction and preparation, acceptance of probable death but determination to take them all down first.

**Key Details:** Camaro build specs (454 big-block 450hp, Muncie 4-speed transmission, reinforced frame), garage at night (single work light, metal-on-metal sounds, cigarette smoke), weapons specifications (Remington 870 12-gauge, .38 Special revolver), test drive locations (Packard Plant roads), Casey's mechanical expertise showing through build quality.

## Chapter 6: "First Blood"

Target Word Count: 1,700 words
POV: Casey McKenna, first person
Setting: Packard Plant area, abandoned factory quarter-mile straightaway

**Purpose:** Casey's first kill—crossing the line from victim to killer, sending message to racing circuit.

**Beats:**

**Dialogue Topics:** Pre-race betting chatter, Tony's cocky trash talk, other racers commenting on Casey (Danny's sister, she's different now), Tony's dying pleas, Casey's internal dialogue justifying the kill.

**Emotional Arc:** Cold determination going in, adrenaline rush during race, moment of hesitation at wreckage seeing Tony dying, then hardening—he killed Danny, this is justice.

**Key Details:** Packard Plant atmosphere (abandoned, broken windows, makeshift starting line, car headlights as illumination), race crowd demographics, Tony Rizzo's appearance, Mustang specifications, concrete barrier impact physics, Casey's physical response to first kill (shaking hands afterward, adrenaline crash).
